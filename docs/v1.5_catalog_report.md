# selene-base v1.5 catalog report

*Per-site breakdown of the 69-site v1.5 catalog with per-criterion scores and Wueller 2026 cross-evaluation. Companion to the README; the README answers "what is selene-base?", this report answers "every site, every score, every comparison — judge for yourself."*

---

## 1. Executive summary

selene-base v1.5 is a multi-criteria habitat-suitability ranker for the lunar south pole. Run end-to-end at 20 m on the LOLA south polar DEM (Barker et al. 2021, `ldem_80s_20m`, 30400 × 30400 pixels), it produces **69 HLS-compliant candidate Artemis III landing sites** distributed across 8 of NASA's 9 down-selected USGS regions. The release ships GPU-accelerated horizon-profile derivation (CuPy on NVIDIA GB10 / Blackwell) and a per-region tiled architecture that fits the 20 m work within ~140 GB of unified memory.

Against [Wueller et al. 2026](https://doi.org/10.1029/2025JE009434) — a peer-reviewed analysis identifying 130 candidate Artemis III landing sites by the same outer methodology (NASA HLS hard filters followed by within-region selection) — **56 of selene's 69 sites (81.2 %) match within 5 km of an in-scope Wueller site, with a median matched-pair distance of 1.76 km**.

This report adds two things the headline number does not:

1. **Per-site, per-criterion breakdown** of all 69 sites, so a reader can audit *why* each site ranked where it did rather than trusting an aggregate.
2. **Wueller-side cross-evaluation:** for each of the 73 in-scope Wueller sites, what does selene's per-criterion stack say at *those* coordinates? This answers the methodological question — "do the two catalogs agree on what makes a *good* cell?" — that the spatial 5 km match-distance does not.

| metric | value |
|---|---:|
| selene v1.5 catalog size | 69 sites across 8 USGS regions |
| selene matched within 5 km of an in-scope Wueller site | **56 / 69 (81.2 %)** |
| Wueller in-scope sites matched within 5 km of a selene site | 44 / 73 (60.3 %) |
| **median matched-pair distance** | **1.76 km** |
| Wueller in-scope sites passing selene's HLS thresholds | **21 / 73 (28.8 %)** |
| selene 69-site median aggregate score | 0.657 |
| Wueller 73-site median aggregate score (at their coords) | 0.596 |
| Δ median aggregate (selene − Wueller) | +0.061 |

The novel finding from cross-evaluation: **selene and Wueller agree closely on which cells are spatially good** (1.76 km median match distance) **but diverge on which cells satisfy the eligibility filters**. 52 of Wueller's 73 in-scope sites would not pass selene's HLS thresholds (slope ≤ 8°, illumination ≥ 0.33, DTE visibility ≥ 0.50), driven primarily by lower illumination at Wueller's coordinates (median 0.43 vs selene's 0.55). Section 5 unpacks this.

## 2. Methodology summary

### Active criteria (default weights)

| criterion | weight | source data | notes |
|---|---:|---|---|
| slope | 0.20 | LOLA `ldem_80s_20m` slope (degrees) | $s = \max(0, 1 - x / 8°)$ |
| illumination | 0.18 | Mazarico average-illumination 65 °S 240 m | linear ramp 0 → 0.70 |
| thermal | 0.15 | Diviner PRP `temp_avg` | Gaussian peak 140 K, σ 30 K |
| ice | 0.15 | Diviner PRP `ice_depth` | linear ramp 0 → 1 m |
| coupling | 0.15 | Mazarico illumination + LOLA slope | multiplicative product of two distance falloffs |
| LOS-to-Earth | 0.15 | LOLA-derived horizon profile + libration sampling | linear ramp 0.20 → 0.50 |
| hazard | 0.05 | Robbins crater catalog (kept zero-weighted in v1.5; criterion is pre-derived but not active by default) | requires Robbins download |
| seismic | 0.0 | Watters scarp catalog (not yet integrated) | will activate once the catalog ships |

Only six criteria contribute to v1.5's aggregate score; the unused two (`hazard`, `seismic`) are skipped via weight renormalisation as documented in the README.

### NASA HLS hard filters (applied per-region after scoring)

| filter | threshold | source |
|---|---|---|
| slope | ≤ 8 ° | NASA HLS Requirements Document |
| buffer to steeper terrain | ≥ 100 m | NASA HLS Requirements Document |
| illumination | ≥ 33 % | Gracy & Lee 2024 (LPSC) |
| DTE visibility | ≥ 50 % | Gracy & Lee 2024 (LPSC) |

At 240 m the 100 m buffer constraint rounds to 0.42 pixels — looser than published. v1.5's per-region tiled ranker re-derives slope and Earth-LOS visibility at 20 m on the per-tile grid, where the buffer becomes a 5-pixel constraint and the published threshold is enforced as written.

### Per-region selection

Within each USGS polygon, the ranker keeps every cell that passes all four HLS filters, then runs greedy non-maximum suppression at 2 km separation to extract up to 10 sites per region by aggregate score.

### Comparison threshold (vs Wueller 2026)

5 km — the upper end of typical regional candidate-site granularity. Not tuned from the agreement result. (See README's "Quantitative comparison against Wueller et al. 2026" for sensitivity to this threshold.)

**What does the score mean?** Each criterion produces a $[0, 1]$ score from raw remote-sensing data (linear ramps for illumination/LOS, Gaussian peak for thermal, distance-falloff product for coupling). The aggregate is $\sum_i w_i \, s_i / \sum_i w_i$ over the criteria with finite score grids — six in v1.5, since `hazard` requires the Robbins catalog (not downloaded in this run) and `seismic` awaits the Watters scarp integration. Weight renormalisation is the standard `weighted_sum` semantics: missing criteria don't drag the score, the remaining weights are rescaled to sum to 1.

The coupling-zero result is structural, not a regression: the coupling criterion encodes "near a PSR *and* near a sunlit ridge" via the multiplicative product of two distance falloffs, but the HLS slope filter (≤ 8 °) excludes both PSR-adjacent and ridge-adjacent terrain, so the conjunction is zero everywhere inside HLS-eligible polygons. This is consistent with the v0.7–v1.0 finding that linear-sum aggregation cannot model NASA's coupling constraint (see README §"v0.7" roadmap entry); the per-region HLS framing the project adopted in v1.3 inherently bypasses coupling rather than relying on it.

### Headline findings from the cross-evaluation

**Median selene aggregate at Wueller's coordinates: 0.596** (vs selene's own catalog median 0.657, Δ +0.061). The two distributions overlap heavily — see plot below — but selene's catalog is consistently above Wueller's at the high end and Wueller's tail extends further down.

[![selene's 69 sites vs Wueller's 73 in-scope sites — aggregate score distribution under selene's 240 m criteria. Selene catalog median 0.657; Wueller median at their coords 0.596.](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Alex0420W/selene-base/main/docs/img/v15_catalog_aggregate_histogram.png)](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Alex0420W/selene-base/main/docs/img/v15_catalog_aggregate_histogram.png)

**HLS compliance is the bigger gap. Only 21 / 73 Wueller in-scope sites pass selene's HLS filters** (slope ≤ 8°, illumination ≥ 0.33, DTE visibility ≥ 0.50). The 52 sites that fail break down as:

- **Illumination shortfall** is the dominant failure mode. Wueller's median illumination at their coords is **0.42 vs selene's 0.55**; many Wueller sites have illumination scores 0–0.20, which translates to raw illumination fractions below 33 % — not HLS-eligible under either project's thresholds, but selene's filter rejects them and Wueller's catalog includes them.
- **DTE visibility** is the secondary failure mode. selene's LOS-to-Earth at Wueller coordinates spans 0–1.0 with a long left tail; ~14 Wueller sites have raw DTE visibility below 0.50.

This is not a methodological *disagreement* between the two projects — both apply the same NASA HLS thresholds. It is more likely a **resolution + threshold-rounding difference**: Wueller works on a 5 m HLS slope raster that distinguishes sub-pixel terrain selene's 240 m illumination raster averages out, and the threshold cutoffs land on different sides of 0.33 / 0.50 once you re-aggregate Wueller's catalog through selene's coarser stack. The 21 Wueller sites that *do* pass selene's HLS rules are exactly the ones in selene's matched cluster.

## 7. Resolution comparison (v1.4.2 vs v1.5)

| metric | v1.4.2 (240 m) | v1.5 (20 m) | Δ |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| selene catalog size | 70 sites | 69 sites | -1 |
| selene matched within 5 km | 56 (80.0 %) | 56 (81.2 %) | +1.2 pp |
| Wueller matched within 5 km | 46 (63.0 %) | 44 (60.3 %) | -2.7 pp |
| **median matched-pair distance** | **1.88 km** | **1.76 km** | **-6.5 %** |
| max matched-pair distance | 4.99 km | 4.81 km | -3.4 % |

Same regions produce the same number of sites at both resolutions, except **Slater Plain**: 9 sites at 240 m, 8 sites at 20 m. The dropped site is one whose 240 m HLS-eligibility band sat at the boundary of the 8 ° slope filter and tightened out at 20 m where the slope is resolved more finely. The eight-site SP catalog at 20 m is a strict subset of the nine-site SP catalog at 240 m by geographic position; selene's NMS at 2 km collapses one previously-distinct cell into a neighbour at the higher resolution.

For the eight regions with sites at both resolutions, six tighten or hold their median matched-pair distance (Haworth 2.09 → 1.77 km, Mons Mouton 2.30 → 1.91 km, MMP 2.38 → 2.03 km, Slater Plain 2.16 → 2.03 km, NR2 1.14 → 1.13 km, Mons Mouton 100 → 100 % matched). NR1 ticks up 1.30 → 1.32 km (within rounding noise of an n=8 sample) and CB drifts 1.94 → 2.04 km — selene's CB sites pick the same rim cluster but Wueller's 5 CB sites are slightly off-axis from selene's 9 sites, an effect 240 m was averaging away.

**Structural outliers persist at fine resolution.** Malapert Massif still has zero HLS-compliant cells; de Gerlache Rim 2 still produces only 2 sites with 0 / 2 matched. Both are genuine terrain divergences, not 240 m sampling artefacts.

The interpretation: **selene's methodology converges between resolutions.** The 81.2 % vs 80.0 % match-rate change is within ±1-site noise; the 1.76 vs 1.88 km median-pair-distance change is the clearest signal that 20 m resolves terrain 240 m was averaging over without changing the catalog's identity.

## 8. Honest limitations

A peer reviewer would flag the following; surfacing them ourselves preempts the criticism.

1. **20 m on LOLA is partially interpolated.** The LOLA south polar 20 m product (Barker et al. 2021) is a gridded interpolation of altimeter shot returns at lower native density. Effective resolution at the south pole is closer to 50–100 m than to 20 m. Sub-pixel terrain features may not be resolved despite the grid resolution, and selene's 20 m HLS slope/buffer constraints inherit the underlying interpolation uncertainty.
2. **The aggregate score is a weighted linear sum.** Selene v1.5 still aggregates criteria via $\sum w_i s_i / \sum w_i$, which cannot model spatial coupling that the multiplicative coupling criterion was designed to address (see README §"v0.7"). The HLS framing bypasses the coupling failure mode by hard-filtering — but a TOPSIS aggregator would let coupling contribute meaningfully where it is non-zero. Planned v1.7.
3. **The seismic criterion is currently unused.** The Watters scarp catalog has not been integrated; sites near scarp traces are not penalised. A site within Apollo-era shallow-moonquake epicentral uncertainty radius would still rank as if no seismic risk existed. Planned v1.8.
4. **The aggregate score map is 240 m, not 20 m.** Per the v1.5 design, per-criterion rasters are not materialised globally at 20 m; the aggregate at each tile is a bilinear upsample of the 240 m product. This makes the per-criterion scores at v1.5 sites and at Wueller-site coordinates measurements of the same 240 m raster — a fair apples-to-apples comparison, but neither is a 20 m per-criterion measurement. The slope, slope-buffer, and Earth-LOS visibility *are* re-derived at 20 m on the per-region tile (so the HLS filters apply at 20 m); illumination, thermal, ice, and coupling stay at 240 m by design.
5. **Wueller is one validation reference.** NASA's actual selection process likely uses additional criteria not visible to either project (operational risk, redundant traverses, NAC stereo-DEM-derived terrain not in either pipeline). 81.2 % agreement with one peer-reviewed reference is meaningful; it is not a guarantee that selene's catalog matches NASA's eventual selection.
6. **The 5 km match threshold is a choice.** Defended in the README as the upper end of typical regional candidate-site granularity, but there is no principled threshold below 5 km for "spatially equivalent". The match-rate at 4 km is 75 %; at 6 km it rises to 86 %. The headline 81.2 % is robust to this choice, but the precise number is not invariant.
